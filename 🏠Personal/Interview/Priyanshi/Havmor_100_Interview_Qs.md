### 21. **How would you measure the ROI of a TV campaign vs. a digital campaign? What are the key differences?**

**Expected Answer**:
- Understanding of different measurement methodologies
- Awareness of TV campaign measurement challenges
- Digital campaign trackability advantages
- Holistic view of both channel contributions
- Attribution modeling

**Ideal Response**: "TV and digital campaigns have fundamentally different measurement approaches due to their nature:

---

**TV CAMPAIGN MEASUREMENT**:

**Challenges**:
- Hard to isolate TV impact (consumer exposed to multiple touchpoints)
- No direct click-through or transaction tracking
- Requires third-party research (expensive and delayed)

**Measurement Approach**:

1. **Brand Lift Studies** (3-week cycles)
   - Pre and post-campaign surveys (1000+ respondents)
   - Track: Aided awareness, unaided awareness, consideration, purchase intent
   - Cost: Rs 3-5 lakh per study
   - ROI Calculation: If awareness lifts from 30% to 45% among target (15pt lift), assign value based on sales elasticity

2. **Sales Correlation**:
   - Track sales in markets/periods when TV runs
   - Compare to baseline (without TV)
   - Challenge: Isolate TV impact from other factors (promotions, seasonality)

3. **Share of Voice (SOV)**:
   - Compare our TV spend to competitors'
   - Track market share changes
   - If we increase SOV 40%→50% and market share +2%, correlation supports ROI

**ROI Calculation (Illustrative)**:
- TV Spend: Rs 25 lakh for 3-month campaign
- Sales Lift: 15% volume increase = Rs 50 lakh incremental revenue
- **Simple ROI**: (Rs 50 lakh - Rs 25 lakh) / Rs 25 lakh = **100% ROI**
- Challenge: Attributing 100% of lift to TV is imprecise

---

**DIGITAL CAMPAIGN MEASUREMENT**:

**Advantages**:
- Every action trackable: impressions, clicks, conversions
- Real-time measurement and optimization
- Clear attribution (often)

**Measurement Approach**:

1. **Performance Metrics** (Real-time dashboards)
   - **Impressions**: Times ad shown
   - **Click-Through Rate (CTR)**: % of impressions that result in clicks
   - **Conversion Rate**: % of clicks that result in purchase/signup
   - **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)**: Campaign cost ÷ conversions
   - **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)**: Revenue ÷ Ad spend

2. **Example (Illustrative)**:
   - Digital Campaign Spend: Rs 40 lakh (4-week campaign)
   - Impressions: 50M
   - CTR: 2% (1M clicks)
   - Conversion Rate: 5% (50K conversions)
   - Average Order Value: Rs 200
   - Revenue: 50K × Rs 200 = Rs 1 crore
   - **ROAS**: Rs 1 crore / Rs 40 lakh = **2.5:1** (Rs 2.50 return per rupee spent)
   - CPA: Rs 40 lakh / 50K = Rs 800 per customer

3. **Attribution Modeling**:
   - Last-click attribution: Gives full credit to final touchpoint
   - Multi-touch attribution: Distributes credit across customer journey
   - Example: Customer sees Instagram ad (20% credit), clicks email (30% credit), purchases via Google Search (50% credit)

4. **Cohort Analysis**:
   - Track repeat purchase rate, customer lifetime value (CLV)
   - Customer acquired via digital often has higher CLV than TV (more targetable audience)

---

**COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS**:

| Metric | TV Campaign | Digital Campaign |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| **Measurement Speed** | Weeks (post-research) | Real-time |
| **Precision** | Estimated (±10-15%) | Exact (tracking codes) |
| **Cost to Measure** | Rs 3-5 lakh per study | Included in platform (free) |
| **Optimization Speed** | Slow (fixed media buy) | Fast (hourly adjustments) |
| **Attribution** | Indirect, inferential | Direct, trackable |
| **Reach** | Mass audience, brand building | Targeted, performance-driven |
| **ROI Clarity** | Implicit (brand equity) | Explicit (immediate returns) |

---

**INTEGRATED MEASUREMENT (Most Realistic)**:

In reality, both channels work together:

1. **Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM)**:
   - Analyze historical sales data against TV spend, digital spend, seasonality, promotions
   - Statistically isolate each channel's contribution
   - Cost: Rs 10-15 lakh for comprehensive model
   - Output: % of sales driven by TV vs. Digital vs. other factors

2. **Example (3-Year Historical Data)**:
   - Regression analysis shows:
     - TV: Contributes 40% of sales lift (strong brand building)
     - Digital: Contributes 35% of sales lift (strong conversion)
     - Seasonality: 15% (summer peak)
     - Promotions: 10%
   - Based on this, allocate marketing budget: TV 40%, Digital 35%, Promo 15%, Contingency 10%

3. **Holistic ROI**:
   - TV may not drive immediate sales but builds brand equity (future value)
   - Digital drives immediate sales but depends on brand awareness (TV builds this)
   - Combined ROI often higher than sum of parts

---

**MY MEASUREMENT PHILOSOPHY FOR HAVMOR**:

1. **Dual Tracking**:
   - Invest in brand lift studies quarterly (TV impact)
   - Real-time digital dashboards (daily)
   - Annual MMM study (integrated view)

2. **Budget Allocation Based on ROI**:
   - TV: Maintain 25% (brand building, mass reach)
   - Digital: Increase to 40% (higher measurable ROI)
   - Monitor and realloc quarterly

3. **Reconciliation**:
   - Ensure TV brand awareness lift converts to digital conversions
   - If brand awareness up but conversions flat → messaging misalignment
   - If conversions up but awareness flat → relying on retained previous awareness (unsustainable)

4. **Long-Term Thinking**:
   - Measure ROI over 12-month horizon, not just campaign period
   - Repeat purchase rate and CLV matter as much as first purchase

For Havmor, I'd aim for: TV ROI ~1.5:1 (brand building), Digital ROI ~3:1 (performance), Combined ROI ~2:1 (holistic marketing effectiveness)."
